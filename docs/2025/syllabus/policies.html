Course Policies

Submission Format

  • Use the provided templates when available

# Course Policies

## Attendance Policy

- **Attendance is mandatory** for this intensive course
- Missing more than one session may result in course failure
- If you must miss a session due to illness or emergency, contact the instructor immediately
- Make-up work may be assigned for excused absences

## Assignment Submission

### Deadlines
- All assignments are due at 9:00 AM on the specified day
- Late submissions will receive a 10% penalty per day
- Extensions may be granted for documented emergencies

### Submission Format
- Submit all assignments via the course management system
- Use the provided templates when available
- File naming convention: `LastName_Assignment#.ext`
- Acceptable formats: .docx, .pdf, .ipynb (for Python notebooks)

## Academic Integrity

### Plagiarism
- All work must be your own
- Properly cite all sources
- Plagiarism detection software will be used
- Violations will result in course failure

### Collaboration
- Discussion with classmates is encouraged
- However, all submitted work must be completed individually
- Clearly indicate if you received help from others
- Group work is only permitted when explicitly stated

## Technology Policy

### Required Technology
- Bring a laptop to every session
- Ensure all required software is installed before Day 1
- Have a backup plan (USB drive, cloud storage)
- Test your setup before the course begins

### Classroom Etiquette
- Laptops should be used for course activities only
- Silence mobile phones
- No recording without permission
- Be respectful during online activities

## Grading

### Grade Distribution
- Hands-on Assignments (4 × 10%): 40%
- Class Participation: 20%
- Final Project: 40%

### Grading Scale
- A: 90-100%
- B: 80-89%
- C: 70-79%
- D: 60-69%
- F: Below 60%

### Participation Grade Criteria
- Active engagement in discussions
- Completion of in-class activities
- Helping classmates when appropriate
- Asking thoughtful questions

## Communication

### Contacting the Instructor
- Email: [instructor email]
- Response time: Within 24 hours on weekdays
- Office hours: By appointment
- Emergency contact: [if applicable]

### Course Announcements
- Check email daily during the course
- Important updates will be posted on the course website
- Join the course Slack/Discord channel [if applicable]

## Accommodations

### Disability Services
- Students with documented disabilities should contact the instructor on Day 1
- Accommodations will be made according to university policy
- Documentation should be provided to [Office Name]

### Language Support
- This course is conducted in English
- International students may use translation tools for understanding
- However, all submissions must be in English
- Extra time may be granted for non-native speakers (discuss with instructor)

## Health and Safety

### COVID-19 Policies
- Follow current university guidelines
- Masks optional/required based on university policy
- Online participation available if quarantined
- Notify instructor immediately if you test positive

### Mental Health
- This is an intensive course; take care of yourself
- University counseling services: [Contact Info]
- Don't hesitate to reach out if you're struggling

## Final Project

### Requirements
- Original research using course methods
- 3,000-5,000 words
- Must include corpus analysis component
- Detailed guidelines in [Final Project](../assignments/final-project/)

### Timeline
- Proposal due: Day 5 (in class)
- Final submission: 2 weeks after course ends
- Feedback provided: Within 1 week of submission

## Course Evaluation

- Anonymous course evaluations will be distributed on Day 5
- Your feedback helps improve the course
- Evaluations do not affect your grade
- Both numerical ratings and written comments are valuable

## Additional Policies

### Recording Policy
- Course sessions may not be recorded without permission
- Instructor may record sessions for absent students
- Recordings are for personal use only

### Guest Speakers
- Treat guest speakers with respect
- Prepare questions in advance
- Guest speaker materials are confidential unless otherwise stated

### Research Ethics
- Any research involving human subjects requires ethics approval
- Corpus data must be used according to license agreements
- Respect copyright and intellectual property

## Questions?

If you have questions about any policy, please don't hesitate to ask. The goal is to create a productive and supportive learning environment for everyone.
